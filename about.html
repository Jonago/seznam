About rays

A ray is, according to the Master DK, “but a name for a particular force or type of energy, with the emphasis upon the quality which that force exhibits.” Rays thus qualify all creation, including the human constitution. Soul, personality, mental body, emotional (astral) body, physical body, all are coloured by one or other of the seven rays. In order to facilitate study and comprehension of the rays, Benjamin Creme’s Master has kindly provided, over the years, requested information on the ray structure and point of evolution of well-known individuals, but for reasons of privacy, never of living people. Due to politeness the initiatory degree has been omitted in the case of Avatars.

The figures in the column named POD (Point of development) refer to the initiate’s exact point of development attained during that life. For example, if an initiate achieved a level half way between the first and second initiation, it is indicated as 1.5.

Nine figures follow the POD; the rays relate, in order, to the soul, personality, mental body, astral body and physical body. The upper figures represent major rays; the lower figures represent sub-rays. The soul does not have a sub-ray. When the original list of 580 names was published in Maitreya’s Mission, Volume One, sub-rays were not given. With the exception of those whose sub-rays have been given since then, these names still do not have sub-rays listed. Following the rays are the dates of birth and death, the country of origin, and the field of endeavour in which the initiate became known. Many of the people on the list were so versatile that it was virtually impossible to classify them in one category. However, the limits of space available left no option. In a few cases, unfortunately, we were not able to find all relevant data; such omissions are noted by a hyphen.

Detailed information about rays can be found in Benjamin Creme's books Maitreya’s Mission Volumes One and Two (MM Vol. I and II) and in Alice Bailey books Esoteric Psychology.


Data

This list includes all the names of initiates which were published in Benjamin Creme's books Maitreya’s Mission Volumes One, Two and Three, as well as those published in Share International between April 1997 and November 2016.

Similar mistake happened with John Paul II, where in Slovenian issue of SI May 2005, AR was wrongly copied as 5th ray instead of 6th.

For Vimala Thakar POD was set as 1.5 in Slovenian SI January/February 2015. But other sources (list of initiates copied by someone else) gives this person POD 1.45. I couldn't check it in original issue, since I don't have it. I left it at 1.45 in this list.

Double entries

For José Martí in SlovenianSI January/February 2008 following rays were published: SR: 2; PR: 4 (6); MR: 3 (7); AR: 4 (6); PBR: 3 (7). POD: 1,6. In SI October 2003 his rays are following: SR: 6; PR: 1 (6); MR: 7 (3); AR: 2 (4); PBR: 3 (7). POD: 1,5

Chögyam Trungpa is listed twice with different POD and rays. It needed to be checked which entry is correct. In SI January/February 2003 his rays are SR: 2; PR: 4 (2); MR: 6 (2); AR: 4 (2); PBR: 7 (3). POD: 2,5

Andrei Tarkowski (Tarkowsky). In Slovenian SI March 2005 his rays are SR: 2; PR: 4 (6); MR: 3 (7); AR: 6 (2); PBR: 3 (7). POD: 2,0

Bruno Gröning - in some other list there was SR: 2; PR: 6 (2); MR: 6 (2); AR: 4 (2); PBR: 3 (7). POD: 2,0. In this list they are as published in SI November 2003.

Considering the double entries. For example: rays of Mani was published in SI September 2011 as SR: 2; PR: 6 (3); MR: 4 (7); AR: 6 (6); PBR: 3 (7). POD: 1,45. In next issue of SI October 2011 appology was written for mistake and corrected with right rays and POD. Rays for Mani were already published earlier, but somehow published again in 2011. We can come to conclusion that the rays and POD for individual which was published first is probably correct record. Except in the case when subrays were added later.
